# 🚗 Drive Mode

---

# 📌 Overview

Drive Mode is the primary gameplay experience for **Spellbound Adventures**.

It is designed for:

* children riding in cars
* low-distraction gameplay
* voice-first interaction
* calm storytelling experiences

The mode prioritizes:

* audio narration
* imagination
* verbal interaction

over traditional visual-heavy gameplay.

---

# 🎯 Design Goals

* Keep children engaged without overstimulation
* Minimize required screen interaction
* Encourage imagination and participation
* Create a safe, parent-friendly experience
* Support short gameplay sessions (5–15 mins)

---

# 🧠 Core Philosophy

Drive Mode should feel like:

* an interactive audiobook
* a guided fantasy adventure
* a playable bedtime story

NOT:

* a fast-paced mobile game
* a visually demanding RPG
* a reaction-heavy action game

---

# 🎮 Gameplay Structure

## Core Loop

1. Narration introduces situation
2. Player hears prompt
3. Player responds using voice
4. System evaluates response
5. Narration reacts
6. Story continues

---

# 🎤 Interaction System

## Voice-First Design

Primary interactions use:

* spoken commands
* spoken choices
* verbal spell casting

Examples:

* "Fire!"
* "Block!"
* "Slash!"
* "Help the villager!"

---

# ✍️ Limited Touch Interaction

Touch interaction should remain optional.

Allowed:

* large buttons
* simple taps
* occasional drawing (tablet mode)

Avoid:

* complex menus
* fast gestures
* small UI targets

---

# 📖 Narration System

Narration is the main storytelling method.

The narrator should:

* speak clearly
* use simple language
* maintain calm pacing
* encourage participation

---

# 🧒 Child Interaction Examples

## Wizard Example

Narrator:

> "The dragon blocks the bridge! Say FIRE to cast a spell!"

Child:

> "FIRE!"

Narrator:

> "A burst of flame shoots forward!"

---

## Swordie Example

Narrator:

> "The goblin attacks! Say BLOCK!"

Child:

> "BLOCK!"

Narrator:

> "You raise your shield just in time!"

---

# 📱 UI Design Principles

## Visual Priorities

* Large readable text
* Minimal clutter
* Calm colors
* Large buttons
* Dark mode friendly

---

## Audio Priorities

* Narration clarity
* Soft ambient sounds
* Positive feedback sounds
* No loud or sudden audio spikes

---

# 🚗 Safety Considerations

Drive Mode should:

* avoid requiring constant screen attention
* support hands-free interaction
* avoid rapid visual changes
* minimize distractions for drivers

---

# ⏱️ Session Structure

Recommended session length:

* 5–15 minutes

Encounters should:

* be short
* be replayable
* support pause/resume

---

# 🎯 MVP Scope

## Included

* Narration text
* Voice prompts
* Basic keyword recognition
* Simple encounter flow

---

## Not Included

* Complex animations
* Multiplayer
* Open world navigation
* Heavy touch interaction

---

Drive Mode focuses on:
